About the Project

NIST Risk Assessment Model

This project demonstrates live calculation of cyber-risk metrics using the NIST methodology. It provides an interactive interface for understanding and evaluating cybersecurity risks in financial terms.

Cybersecurity risk management is essential for organizations to protect their assets, data, and reputation. By quantifying risks in financial terms, businesses can make informed decisions about security investments and priorities.

SLE (Single Loss Expectancy)

The monetary value expected from the occurrence of a risk on a single asset.

EF (Exposure Factor)

The percentage of asset value that would be lost if a risk occurs.

ARO (Annualized Rate of Occurrence)

The estimated frequency with which a threat is expected to occur in a year.

ALE (Annualized Loss Expectancy)

The expected monetary loss per year due to a specific risk.

Conclusion & Research Value

The Cyber Risk Assessment Dashboard provides an interactive platform for understanding and applying the NIST risk assessment model. By visualizing the relationship between asset value, exposure factor, and attack frequency, users can gain insights into the financial impact of cybersecurity risks.

This tool improves understanding of the NIST risk model by making abstract concepts tangible through interactive controls and real-time calculations. It serves as a valuable educational resource for cybersecurity students and a practical tool for professionals involved in risk management.

The dashboard's ability to simulate different scenarios and evaluate financial cyber risk in real-time makes it a powerful instrument for training, decision-making, and strategic planning in cybersecurity risk management.
